About CUP Technology

Compressive Ultrafast Photography (CUP) is a revolutionary imaging technique that can capture dynamic events at extremely high frame rates, up to trillions of frames per second. It combines concepts from compressed sensing and computational imaging to break traditional limitations of cameras.

How It Works

  • Spatial Encoding: Using a Digital Micromirror Device (DMD) to spatially encode the scene
  • Temporal Shearing: Streaking the encoded image in time through a streak camera
  • Computational Reconstruction: Recovering the original video frames using compressed sensing algorithms
